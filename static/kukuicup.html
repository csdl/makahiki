Kukui Cup:Lights Out, Game On!

About the Kukui Cup
  • What: A competition in freshman residence halls to see which floor can use the least energy over a 3 week period.
  • Who: Freshmen living in the Hale Aloha towers (Ilima, Lehua, Mokihana)
  • When: Spring 2011

  • The Kukui Cup is the first student housing energy competition on the UH Mānoa campus. Each Lounge (pair of floors) in the Hale Aloha towers will try to use the least energy during the competition. The competition also seeks to increase the participants’ ‘energy literacy’ through a jam-packed schedule of events, commitments, and activities all coordinated through the competition website. Participants earn Kukui Nut points as they attend events and engage in activities, competing to see who can earn the most points. Valuable prizes will be awarded for in both the energy and Kukui Nut point competitions. All in all, it should be a fun way to make the residence halls more sustainable and energy efficient.
